# British Ministry of Magic Portal

## Project Vision

> **"A living magical world where players don't simply play a game—they live as Ministry employees."**

---

# 1. Vision

The goal of this project is not to create another Harry Potter fan game.

The goal is to build a believable digital workplace inside the Wizarding World.

Players should eventually stop feeling like they are controlling a character.

Instead, they should feel:

> **"I work for the British Ministry of Magic."**

Every feature should reinforce this feeling.

---

# 2. Core Philosophy

The Hidden Files is **not the main product.**

It is one department inside a much larger world.

The true product is:

# British Ministry of Magic Portal

A persistent online magical workplace.

Players create one employee account.

That employee continues to grow for years.

---

# 3. Core Experience

The experience is not solving mysteries.

The experience is **living as a magical government employee.**

Investigation

Research

Promotion

Daily work

Internal communication

Friendships

Department culture

All of these together create immersion.

Mysteries become only one part of daily work.

---

# 4. The Player

The player is never "the hero."

The player is an employee.

A real officer inside the Ministry.

Players begin as junior employees.

Over time they receive promotions,

higher clearance,

new departments,

and more responsibility.

---

# 5. Wizard Identity

Every employee owns a permanent magical identity.

This identity includes:

• Wand

• House

• Patronus

• Blood Status

• Magical Aptitude

• Preferred Spell Types

• Psychological Profile

• Department Compatibility

These are never random.

Instead,

they are determined through an intelligent psychological analysis.

Players should react with:

> "How did it know me so well?"

---

# 6. AI Identity Analysis

Instead of simple quizzes,

players complete an onboarding assessment.

The system analyzes:

• personality

• moral judgement

• decision making

• emotional tendencies

• leadership

• curiosity

• responsibility

• risk tolerance

Using these,

AI recommends:

House

Patronus

Wand

Department

Magical specialization

Clearance path

The result should feel like a magical hiring process,

not a personality test.

---

# 7. Daily Ministry Life

Every login should feel like arriving at work.

Example:

Unread Owl Mail

Today's Assignment

Daily Prophet

Department Notices

Emergency Incidents

Meeting Requests

Promotion Progress

Training Tasks

Recovered Archives

The player should naturally think:

> "Let's see what's waiting for me today."

---

# 8. Living World

The Ministry never sleeps.

News changes.

NPCs change.

Departments react.

Cases appear.

Investigations evolve.

Players influence future stories.

The world continues even when the player logs out.

---

# 9. Community

Other players are not competitors.

They are fellow Ministry employees.

Players can:

Share reports

Discuss investigations

Send Owl Mail

Cooperate on difficult cases

Recommend evidence

Work together during Ministry-wide incidents

---

# 10. Long-Term Progression

Progress should take months or years.

Promotion

Department transfer

Security clearance

Special certifications

Legendary investigations

Career achievements

Retirement records

Every account becomes a long-term story.

---

# 11. The Hidden Files

The Hidden Files remains the Archive Division.

It becomes the Ministry's primary archive investigation system.

CASE ZERO is only the beginning.

Hundreds of investigations may eventually exist.

---

# 12. Success

The project succeeds when players no longer say:

"I played a Harry Potter game."

Instead they say:

> "I logged into work today."

That feeling is the true goal of the project.
