# British Ministry of Magic Portal
## World Building

## 1. Core Setting

This project takes place inside the British Ministry of Magic.

Players are not visitors, heroes, or chosen ones.

Players are Ministry employees.

The Ministry is presented as a living workplace with departments, records, notices, mail, cases, promotions, duties, conflicts, and secrets.

The world should feel bureaucratic, magical, old, secretive, and alive.

---

## 2. Main Structure

The Ministry Portal is divided into departments.

Each department has its own purpose, culture, permissions, documents, and storylines.

### Core Departments

- Archive Division
- Auror Office
- Internal Affairs
- Department of Mysteries
- Magical Law Enforcement
- Magical Creatures Division
- International Magical Cooperation
- Wizengamot Administration
- Owl Mail Office
- Training & Certification Office

---

## 3. Archive Division

Archive Division is the home of **The Hidden Files**.

Its role is to recover, restore, classify, and protect magical records.

Archive Division handles:

- damaged records
- sealed case files
- memory-corrupted documents
- forbidden indexes
- hidden evidence
- historical magical incidents

### Current Major Case

- CASE-ZERO
- CASE-OMEGA
- BOX17
- The Lethe Archive

Archive Division should feel quiet, dusty, dangerous, and deeply secretive.

---

## 4. Auror Office

Auror Office handles active magical threats.

It is more direct and urgent than Archive Division.

Auror Office handles:

- dark wizard activity
- cursed artefacts
- magical attacks
- wanted persons
- emergency investigations

Its culture is practical, intense, and action-focused.

---

## 5. Internal Affairs

Internal Affairs investigates the Ministry itself.

It handles corruption, missing records, unauthorized evidence transfer, and employee misconduct.

Internal Affairs should feel cold, formal, suspicious, and uncomfortable.

No one fully trusts Internal Affairs.

---

## 6. Department of Mysteries

The Department of Mysteries handles unknown magical phenomena.

It should feel strange, abstract, and unsettling.

It deals with:

- time
- memory
- death
- prophecy
- identity
- impossible rooms
- unstable magic

This department should unlock later and feel more dangerous than ordinary Ministry work.

---

## 7. Daily Life Systems

The Ministry Portal should include ordinary work-life systems, not only mysteries.

Important daily systems:

- Clock In
- Owl Mail
- Daily Prophet
- Department Notices
- Today's Assignment
- Training Tasks
- Promotion Progress
- Employee Profile
- Personal Desk
- Case Inbox
- Ministry Calendar

The goal is to make players feel like they are returning to work.

---

## 8. Employee Identity

Every player has a permanent employee identity.

This includes:

- Employee Name
- Employee ID
- Department
- Rank
- Clearance Level
- Date Joined
- Reputation
- Active Cases
- Completed Training
- Promotion History

This identity should persist over time.

---

## 9. Wizard Identity

Each employee also has a magical identity.

This includes:

- House
- Wand
- Patronus
- Blood Status
- Magical Aptitude
- Preferred Spell Type
- Department Compatibility
- Psychological Profile

These should never feel random.

They should be assigned through a meaningful analysis system.

The player should feel personally understood.

---

## 10. Progression

Players begin as junior Ministry employees.

Over time they gain:

- higher clearance
- new departments
- new case access
- advanced training
- reputation
- promotions
- hidden files
- special assignments

Progression should feel like a career, not a game level.

---

## 11. Living World Events

The world should change over time.

Examples:

- Dragon incident reported in London
- New Daily Prophet article published
- Department memo issued
- Internal Affairs investigation opened
- Owl Mail from NPC
- Emergency assignment added
- Case file updated
- Ministry-wide alert triggered

The Ministry should feel alive even when the player is offline.

---

## 12. Core Tone

The tone should be:

- official
- mysterious
- bureaucratic
- magical
- slightly unsettling
- immersive
- document-driven
- emotionally personal

The Ministry should not feel like a fantasy theme park.

It should feel like a real government workplace that happens to be magical.

---

## 13. Key Principle

The player should not feel like they are playing a Harry Potter game.

The player should feel:

> I am a Ministry employee.

Everything in the world should support that feeling.
